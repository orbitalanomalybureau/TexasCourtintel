# Contractor IP Assignment + Confidentiality (Draft Filled)

> Draft for Texas Court Intel LLC. Have Texas counsel review before execution.

This Agreement is made between **Texas Court Intel LLC** ("Company") and **[Contractor Name]** ("Contractor") effective **[Date]**.

## 1. Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" includes all non-public information regarding Company technology, source code, product plans, pricing, customer data, business methods, prompts/workflows, and trade secrets.

## 2. Non-Disclosure & Use Restrictions
Contractor shall:
- keep Confidential Information strictly confidential,
- use it only to perform services for Company,
- not disclose to third parties without written authorization,
- protect it with at least reasonable care,
- not copy or export confidential datasets beyond approved work systems.

## 3. Work Product Ownership / Assignment
All work product created in connection with services for Company, including code, documentation, designs, prompts, data schemas, and related IP, is deemed work made for hire to the extent allowed by law. To the extent not work made for hire, Contractor irrevocably assigns all rights, title, and interest to Company.

## 4. Pre-Existing IP
Contractor retains ownership of pre-existing IP listed in Exhibit A. Contractor grants Company a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free license to use any pre-existing IP incorporated into deliverables.

## 5. Security Requirements
Contractor shall:
- use Company-approved accounts and MFA,
- avoid storing Company secrets in plaintext,
- avoid uploading Company confidential data to public AI tools without written approval,
- promptly report security incidents,
- comply with Company access/offboarding procedures.

## 6. Return/Deletion
Upon request or termination, Contractor shall promptly return or securely delete Company Confidential Information and certify deletion in writing.

## 7. No Publicity
Contractor may not publicly reference Company projects, clients, courts data methods, or roadmap details without prior written consent.

## 8. Injunctive Relief
Contractor acknowledges unauthorized use/disclosure may cause irreparable harm; Company may seek injunctive relief in addition to other remedies.

## 9. Governing Law / Venue
Texas law governs this Agreement; venue in **[Travis/Bexar/Other County], Texas** unless otherwise required.

## 10. Survival
Confidentiality and IP assignment obligations survive termination.
